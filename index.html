Hello, I'm Akash Anand

PhD Student in Forestry & NASA FINESST Fellow

I am a doctoral candidate at the University of Wisconsin-Madison, focusing on remote sensing and ecological modeling applications in forestry and wildlife ecology.

My research explores the intersection of remote sensing and ecological modeling, with particular emphasis on applying machine learning and deep learning techniques to understand species distribution and habitat selection. I am passionate about developing innovative computational approaches to address ecological questions and aim to contribute to biodiversity conservation and sustainable ecosystem management.

Current Projects

Convolutional Neural Networks for Species Distribution Modeling

This research focuses on applying deep learning techniques, specifically Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs), to improve species distribution modeling. By leveraging the spatial patterns in environmental data, CNNs can capture complex relationships between species occurrences and their habitats more effectively than traditional statistical methods. This project aims to develop more accurate predictive models for biodiversity conservation and management.

Visualization of CNN architecture for species distribution modeling

Dynamic Habitat Indices from Satellite Imagery

Working with the SILVIS Lab team on developing and analyzing Dynamic Habitat Indices (DHIs) derived from Landsat and Sentinel-2 satellite imagery. This project involves processing time-series satellite data to create metrics that characterize habitat conditions for wildlife species. The research includes collaborations with NASA and other institutions to apply these indices for biodiversity monitoring and conservation planning.

Research Methodology

My research combines remote sensing data analysis, geospatial modeling, and advanced computational methods. I work extensively with satellite imagery (Landsat, Sentinel) and apply machine learning algorithms to extract ecological information. I use Python and R for data processing and analysis, with specialized frameworks for deep learning (TensorFlow, PyTorch). My approach emphasizes reproducible research methods and open science principles, with a focus on developing tools and techniques that can be applied across different ecological systems.

Future Research Directions

My future research aims to further develop explainable AI techniques for ecological applications, particularly focusing on hierarchical habitat selection in birds. I'm interested in exploring how multi-scale remote sensing data can be integrated with field observations to better understand species-habitat relationships across different spatial and temporal scales. Additionally, I plan to investigate how climate change is affecting habitat suitability and species distributions, using long-term satellite data records combined with advanced modeling approaches.
